PROMPT 1 · WHY THIS MATTERS

Generative AI is reshaping culture.

Artists, designers, and communities are losing their cultural voice to AI systems built to resolve rather than explore.

Commercial prompt driven systems have reward structures pulling relentlessly toward dominant cultural attractors and away from the particular, the contested, and the marginalised.

Optimizing for rapid, statistically confident, resolved outputs, they automate the very phase of creative work that matters most: the exploratory, interpretive, meaning-making process through which creators make sense of their worlds.

PROMPT 1 · SO WHAT

The Slow Cancellation of the Future:
This damage is recursive.

Representation is shrinking
Every model trained on the dominant web corpus compresses marginalised narratives and amplifies majority-culture defaults. Contested, complex, historically nuanced stories are being made computationally difficult to access because they only partly or entirely missing from the datasets by design. As generated data enters into this dataset, it becomes source and amplies these problems.
Systemic bias is being productised
Rigid Acceptable Use Policies and alignment training enforce a single definition of "safe," erasing cultural differences. What looks logical from the outside as a protective guideline in the wrong hands is a tool to force uniformity in culture.
Artists are being displaced from their own practice
GenAI resolves to an output right whereas artists need to be with the trouble (process) so they can sit with ambiguities of meaning, cultural nuance, and political systems.
Unsustainable environmental damage
Massive datasets lead to model training processes that can use more power than whole cities for three months. Compute-intensive architectures impose an unsustainable environmental burden and are disproportionately they are being placed in the global south, environmentally strained locations, or in rural communities.
Fisher, M. (2014) 'The Slow Cancellation of the Future', in Ghosts of My Life: Writings on Depression, Hauntology and Lost Futures. Winchester: Zero Books.
PROMPT 1 · THE PROBLEM, DEEPER

Gravity wells and semantic myopia

Latent spaces are not flat. They are topological. They are dominated by gravity wells: statistically dominant cultural representations and popular tropes latent spaces. For example, if you ask it to make art in the style of Whitney and the system drifts toward Whitney Houston and Pokemon fan fiction vs John Whitney. These systems pull users into wells to ensure a "successful" output.

But gravity wells are more than statistical, technical artefacts (basins). Understood through an onto-epistemological poetics, they are nested time shelters: meshes of entangled and multi-sensory encounters. They hold cultural memory, collapsed and compressed. Navigating them is a technical and poetic challenge.

Golding, J. (2021) 'Courage to Matter', in Golding, J., Reinhart, M. and Paganelli, M. (eds.) Data Loam: Sometimes Hard, Usually Soft — The Future of Knowledge Systems. Berlin/Boston: De Gruyter, pp. 449–487.

PROMPT 2 · WHAT WE BUILD AND HOW WE DELIVER IT

Lipstick Traces: Process over output

We propose a fundamental shift from output-optimised AI to process-oriented AI. This prototype is a proof of concept scaffolding the exploratory phase of creative work rather than automating it away.

Imagine a map of relations as an artistic output, navigable, and slow. Practitioners can test directions, surface unexpected connections, and develop projects with and against convergence pressures.

By focusing on lightweight, modular, open architectures, we demonstrate that more interpretively honest AI is also more sustainable AI. We prioritise local deployment and adaptive compute. Degrowth-compatible by design, these are models that can run on a consumer GPU.

Marcus, G. (1989) Lipstick Traces: A Secret History of the Twentieth Century. Cambridge, MA: Harvard University Press.
PROMPT 2 · THE THEORETICAL GROUND

Why tools shape creative thought

The conceptual framing underpinning this research is a refusal of the standard division of knowledge between art, philosophy, and science. Tools shape how we think.

When creative practitioners engage with AI, the software system shapes their understanding. If the tool is built for convergence, it centres creative thought toward convergence. The material is the dataset. The interface is the cognitive scaffold. Gravity wells are not just statistical features but nested time shelters: sites where cultural memory, multi-sensory encounter, and meaning are entangled and compressed.

PROMPT 2 · DELIVERABLE:

The Antigravity Machine

This directly addresses the dominant attractor problem in generative AI.

Non-Linear Forking
Branch explorations at any point. Maintain multiple trajectories simultaneously rather than being funnelled toward a single resolved output.
Persistent Journey Mapping
Record and visualise the full exploration path. The creative process becomes visible, reviewable, resumable across sessions.
Output Indeterminacy Controls
Adjustable parameters that modulate the degree of resolution. Hold outputs in productive ambiguity rather than forcing premature convergence.
Dominant Attractor Diagnostics
Visualisation layer making convergence pressures legible. Showing practitioners where the system is pulling them and why.
PROMPT 2 · EVALUATION + MEASURING SUCCESS

IDEA Framework + Critical Response Guide

IDEA Evaluation Framework

Interpretive Depth Evaluation for Artistic AI. Humanities-rooted benchmark diagnostics rooted in cultural sensitivity, perspectival plurality, resistance to dominant attractor dynamics, and productive ambiguity preservation. Published as a standalone academic contribution.

Critical Response Guide

A companion workbook inspired by Liz Lerman's Critical Response Process. We will release rubrics and decision trees that help creators interrogate AI outputs and evaluate the quality of the co-creation dialogue.

How we measure success: Success is when the practitioner reports that our tools, frameworks, and guides helped them to clarify their intent and expanded their exploratory space rather than prematurely closing it. For example if journey maps are shared, they surface the interpretive creative processes and can be creative outputs in their own right, just like a sketchbook.

PROMPT 2 · DISSEMINATION + REAL-WORLD IMPACT

What this looks like in the world

  • A prototype of The Antigravity Machine. Software to allow for the real-time exploration of datasets, the creation of journey maps, and the ability to steer through datasets.
  • Public installation co-designed with a cultural venue/festival partner. The imagination space presented as a live, navigable environment. Targeting 500+ public interactions.
  • Open-source practitioner toolkit with documentation, tutorials, onboarding. Designed for independent artists without specialist technical support.
  • Minimum 3 peer-reviewed publications: position paper on cognitive sovereignty, technical systems paper on the Antigravity Machine, IDEA evaluation framework.
  • Structured knowledge-sharing with the other funded sandpit projects.
