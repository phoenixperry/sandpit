PROMPT 1 · WHY THIS MATTERS

Generative AI is reshaping culture.

Artists, designers, and communities are losing their cultural voice to AI systems built to resolve rather than explore.

Commercial prompt driven systems have reward structures pulling relentlessly toward dominant cultural attractors and away from the particular, the contested, and the marginalised.

Optimizing for rapid, statistically confident, resolved outputs, they automate the very phase of creative work that matters most: the exploratory, interpretive, meaning-making process through which artists develop original ideas and surface marginalised narratives.

This is not a set of separate technical problems. It is a single systemic failure, rooted in one foundational design assumption: that the purpose of AI is to resolve rather than to explore.

PROMPT 1 · SO WHAT

The Slow Cancellation of the Future:
This damage is recursive.

Representation is shrinking
Every model trained on the dominant web corpus compresses marginalised narratives and amplifies majority-culture defaults. The more these tools are used, the narrower the representational space becomes. Contested, complex, historically nuanced stories are being made computationally inaccessible because they are missing from the datasets by design.
Systemic bias is being productised
Rigid Acceptable Use Policies and alignment training enforce a single definition of "safe," erasing cultural differences. This is not a protective guideline but rather a process that forces uniformity in culture. In the wrong hands, it acts as a type of censorship, disguising pre-existing structural inequality behind the appearance of statistical certainty. For example, I am shadowbanned on X for speaking against the stoning of LGBTQAI+ people by the Sultan of Brunei
Artists are being displaced from their own practice
GenAI resolves to an output right whereas artists need to be with the trouble (process) so they can sit with ambiguities of meaning, cultural nuance, and political systems. This stands to dramatically reduce critical inquiry.
Unsustainable environmental damage
Massive datasets lead to model training processes that can use more power than whole cities for three months. Compute-intensive architectures impose an unsustainable environmental burden.Disproportionately, these data centres are being placed in the global south, environmentally strained locations, or in rural communities.
Mark Fisher, "The Slow Cancellation of the Future," Ghosts of My Life: Writings on Depression, Hauntology and Lost Futures, Zero Books, 2014
PROMPT 1 · THE PROBLEM, DEEPER

Gravity wells and semantic myopia

Latent spaces are not flat. They are topological. They are dominated by gravity wells: statistically dominant cultural representations and popular tropes. Write about an artist named Whitney and the system drifts toward Whitney Houston and Pokemon fan fiction. These systems pull users into wells to ensure a "successful" output. Which really means: a statistically probable one.

We don't view gravity wells as inherently bad. They are contextual landmarks. But practitioners need mechanisms to choose how to engage with them or actively construct trajectories away.

PROMPT 2 · WHAT WE BUILD AND HOW WE DELIVER IT

Lipstick Traces: Process over output

We propose a fundamental shift from output-optimised AI to process-oriented AI. This prototype is a proof of concept scaffolding the exploratory phase of creative work rather than automating it away.

Imagine a map of relations as an artistic output, navigable, and slow. Practitioners can test directions, surface unexpected connections, and develop projects with and against convergence pressures. Generating work through a series of dataset sketches, creators reclaim their cultural, political, and storytelling roles.

By focusing on lightweight, modular, open architectures, we demonstrate that more interpretively honest AI is also more sustainable AI. We prioritise local deployment and adaptive compute. Degrowth-compatible by design, these are models that can run on a consumer GPU, not a data centre. This is a direct technical alternative to the dominant prompt-based data harvesting paradigm of companies such as Google and OpenAI.

Greil Marcus, Lipstick Traces: A Secret History of the Twentieth Century
PROMPT 2 · THE THEORETICAL GROUND

Why tools shape creative thought

The theory underpinning this research embodies Material Engagement Theory. It tells us that cognition does not happen solely in the head. It extends through our engagement with materials and tools. Tools shape how we think.

When creative practitioners engage with AI, the tool is not neutral. If the tool is built for convergence, it shapes creative thought toward convergence. The material is the dataset. The interface is the cognitive scaffold.

Kirsh and Maglio's concept of epistemic action reinforces this: actions taken not to achieve a goal directly, but to change our cognitive relationship to a problem. Sketching to discover. Moving pieces around to think. Current AI tools collapse this by jumping straight to output. Our prototype will restore it. Through forking, journey mapping, and navigating gravity wells, we create tools for creative practice that create sticky cohesions within datasets.

Lambros Malafouris, How Things Shape the Mind: A Theory of Material Engagement, MIT Press, 2013

Golding, Johnny; Reinhart, Martin; and Paganellli, Mattia, eds. 2021. Data Loam: Sometimes Hard, Usually Soft. The Future of Knowledge Systems. Berlin/Boston: De Gruyter.

David Kirsh and Paul Maglio, "On Distinguishing Epistemic from Pragmatic Action," Cognitive Science, 18(4), 513–549, 1994

PROMPT 2 · DELIVERABLE: WP2.1

The Anti-Gravity Tool Prototype

This directly addresses the dominant attractor problem in generative AI.

Non-Linear Forking
Branch explorations at any point. Maintain multiple trajectories simultaneously rather than being funnelled toward a single resolved output.
Persistent Journey Mapping
Record and visualise the full exploration path. The creative process becomes visible, reviewable, resumable across sessions. The journey map IS the provenance record.
Output Indeterminacy Controls
Adjustable parameters that modulate the degree of resolution. Hold outputs in productive ambiguity rather than forcing premature convergence.
Dominant Attractor Diagnostics
Visualisation layer making convergence pressures legible. Showing practitioners where the system is pulling them and why. Turning an opaque statistical process into a navigational environment.
PROMPT 2 · DELIVERABLE: WP2.2

The Latent Storytelling Framework

Latent space is not a style selector. It is a narrative environment. WP2.2 treats it as one.

Cultural Neighbourhood Mapping
Identify, name, and navigate culturally specific representational regions through deliberate compositional movement. Latent space as a topology of meaning, not a feature vector.
Narrative Threading
Compose intentional trajectories through meaning space. Connect representational neighbourhoods into coherent creative narratives that preserve specificity rather than averaging across them.
Contestation Surfacing
Identify where the latent space encodes cultural power asymmetries. Where marginalised representations are compressed. Where dominant ones are over-represented. Make it legible. Make it navigable.
Meta-Localisation
Cohorting of conceptual pathways that lets users map plural gravity wells and control thematic drift through zeitgeist. Inspiration without plagiarism or appropriation.
PROMPT 2 · EVALUATION + MEASURING SUCCESS

IDEA Framework + Critical Response Guide

WP2.3: IDEA Evaluation Framework

Interpretive Depth Evaluation for Artistic AI. Humanities-rooted benchmarks for cultural sensitivity, perspectival plurality, resistance to dominant attractor dynamics, and productive ambiguity preservation. Published as a standalone academic contribution with worked examples and baseline data.

WP3.2: Critical Response Guide

A companion workbook, more Gamemaster's Guide than user manual. Inspired by Liz Lerman's Critical Response Process. Rubrics and decision trees that help creators interrogate AI outputs and evaluate the quality of the co-creation dialogue. Works with any generative interpretive tool, not just ours. Comparative analysis built in.

How we measure success: Success is when the practitioner reports that our tools, frameworks, and guides helped them to clarify their intent and expanded their exploratory space rather than prematurely closing it. For example if journey maps are shared, they surface the interpretive creative processes.

PROMPT 3 · THE HUMANITIES-DRIVEN TECHNICAL ADVANCE

Philosophy and Critical Theory as generative design logic

The humanities contribution here is not post-hoc critical commentary applied after the engineering is done. It is foundational architectural constraint and our artistic lineage. Our tools, design patterns, and evaluation methods are a collective ecosystem which are will be rooted in crip positionality, feminist understanding, decolonial data, queer theory, and post-captialism technology.

Feminist and crip theory are not supplementary lenses applied after the fact. They are load-bearing design constraints. The curb cut effect demonstrates that access-driven design produces better systems for everyone. Crip technoscience reframes disability not as deficit but as expertise in navigating hostile infrastructure. Feminist epistemology insists on situated knowledge: who builds the tool, from what body, under what conditions. These commitments shape our interface logic, our evaluation criteria, and our refusal to treat "universal" defaults as neutral.

Post-capitalism, hauntology, and science fiction provide the temporal and political imagination. Fisher's hauntology names the cultural condition we are building against: a present haunted by futures that never arrived, flattened by platform capitalism into eternal recombination of the already known. Speculative fiction has always been the laboratory for rehearsing alternative social orders. Our prototype operationalises this: latent space as a site for constructing futures that the dominant paradigm has foreclosed, not merely retrieving pasts it has already digested.

PROMPT 3 · We are the Chimeras: Authentic TRANSDISCIPLINARY ENRICHMENT

Each discipline is changed

The mutual enrichment here is bidirectional and has genuine epistemic consequence.

Technical → Humanities: The implementation surfaces empirical evidence about how dominant attractors function, how communities navigate contested representational spaces, and how latent space topology encodes political power asymmetries.

Humanities → Technical: The requirement for computational precision and testability generates new knowledge within the humanities disciplines themselves. Concepts like selective distortion and epistemic erasure become computational design constraints rather than remaining descriptive theoretical categories. The software system and interface are changed by the theory.

This is the test: Each discipline is substantively changed by its encounter with the other. That is the condition of genuine transdisciplinary research. Not interdisciplinary collaboration in name only. Neither a humanities nor a technical AI team could achieve this independently. We are the Chimeras and we are distinctly positioned to see what is not apparent.
