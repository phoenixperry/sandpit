PROMPT 1 · THE PROBLEM AND THE STAKES

Current AI tools drive towards dominate cultural narratives

Artists, designers, and communities are losing their cultural voice to AI systems built to resolve rather than explore.

Commerical prompt driven systems have reward structures pulling relentlessly toward dominant cultural attractors and away from the particular, the contested, and the marginalised.

This is happening because poplar GenAI models optimise for rapid, statistically confident, resolved outputs. They automate the very phase of creative work that matters most: the exploratory, iterative, meaning-making process through which artists develop original ideas and surface marginalised narratives.

This is not a set of separate technical problems. It is a single systemic failure, rooted in one foundational design assumption: that the purpose of AI is to resolve rather than to explore.

Arts scholarship has been almost entirely excluded from how these systems are designed. Creative practitioners are being economically undermined by tools that were never built for them. The environmental cost of compute-intensive, single-output architectures remains unaddressed. All of this is compounding.

PROMPT 1 · THE PROBLEM, DEEPER

Gravity wells and semantic myopia

Latent spaces are not flat. They are topological. They are dominated by gravity wells: statistically dominant cultural representations and popular tropes. Write about a character named Steve and the system drifts toward Captain America fan fiction. These systems pull users into wells to ensure a "successful" output. Which really means: a statistically probable one.

Semantic Myopia

Platformed AI relies on rigid Acceptable Use Policies and alignment training that fundamentally flatten culture. By enforcing universally "safe" outputs, these systems erase the margins. Contested, complex, historically nuanced narratives become computationally inaccessible.

Cognitive Sovereignty

The current paradigm is built on delegation: the user prompts, the system takes over. This strips creators of their right to govern the trajectory, pace, and context of their creative labour. AI needs to operate as an interpretive assistant, not a mechanical delegate.

We don't view gravity wells as inherently bad. They are contextual landmarks. But practitioners need mechanisms to choose whether to engage with them or actively construct trajectories away.

PROMPT 2 · WHAT WE BUILD AND HOW WE DELIVER IT

Process over output

We propose a fundamental shift from output-optimised AI to process-oriented AI. We are building prototypes that scaffold the exploratory phase of creative work rather than automating it away.

Our tools make the exploratory process visible, navigable, and slow. Practitioners can test directions, surface unexpected connections, and develop projects longitudinally without convergence pressure. We reconceptualise generative AI not as a production system but as a cognitive scaffolding system.

By focusing on lightweight, modular, open architectures, we demonstrate that more interpretively honest AI is also more sustainable AI. Local deployment. Adaptive compute. Degrowth-compatible by design. Models that can run on a consumer GPU, not a data centre. This is a direct technical alternative to the dominant prompt-based paradigm. Not merely a critique of it.

PROMPT 2 · THE THEORETICAL GROUND

Why tools shape creative thought

This is not just an engineering project. The theoretical foundation matters. Material Engagement Theory (Malafouris) tells us that cognition does not happen solely in the head. It extends through our engagement with materials and tools. Tools shape how we think.

When creative practitioners engage with AI, the tool is not neutral. If the tool is built for convergence, it shapes creative thought toward convergence. The material is the dataset. The machine is the cognitive scaffold. That means the design of the tool is a design decision about cognition itself.

Kirsh and Maglio's concept of epistemic action reinforces this: actions taken not to achieve a goal directly, but to change our cognitive relationship to a problem. Sketching to discover. Moving pieces around to think. Creative practice IS epistemic action. Current AI tools collapse this by jumping straight to output. Our system restores it. Forking, journey mapping, navigating gravity wells: these are the epistemic actions through which artists think-through-the-tool.

My PhD work on InteractML explored exactly this: how embodied interaction with ML systems transforms creative cognition when artists can train models through their own movement. The Antigravity Machine extends that logic into generative AI.

PROMPT 2 · DELIVERABLE: WP2.1

The Anti-Gravity Navigation Toolkit

This directly addresses the dominant attractor problem in generative AI.

Non-Linear Forking
Branch explorations at any point. Maintain multiple trajectories simultaneously rather than being funnelled toward a single resolved output.
Persistent Journey Mapping
Record and visualise the full exploration path. The creative process becomes visible, reviewable, resumable across sessions. The journey map IS the provenance record.
Output Indeterminacy Controls
Adjustable parameters that modulate the degree of resolution. Hold outputs in productive ambiguity rather than forcing premature convergence.
Dominant Attractor Diagnostics
Visualisation layer making convergence pressures legible. Showing practitioners where the system is pulling them and why. Turning an opaque statistical process into a navigational environment.

Lightweight LoRA fine-tuning, adaptive compute, modular API. Consumer GPU / cloud-free local deployment.

PROMPT 2 · DELIVERABLE: WP2.2

The Latent Storytelling Framework

Latent space is not a style selector. It is a narrative environment. WP2.2 treats it as one.

Cultural Neighbourhood Mapping
Identify, name, and navigate culturally specific representational regions through deliberate compositional movement. Latent space as a topology of meaning, not a feature vector.
Narrative Threading
Compose intentional trajectories through meaning space. Connect representational neighbourhoods into coherent creative narratives that preserve specificity rather than averaging across them.
Contestation Surfacing
Identify where the latent space encodes cultural power asymmetries. Where marginalised representations are compressed. Where dominant ones are over-represented. Make it legible. Make it navigable.
Meta-Localisation
Cohorting of conceptual pathways that lets users map plural gravity wells and control thematic drift through zeitgeist. Inspiration without plagiarism or appropriation.
PROMPT 2 · EVALUATION + MEASURING SUCCESS

IDEA Framework + Critical Response Guide

WP2.3: IDEA Evaluation Framework

Interpretive Depth Evaluation for Artistic AI. Humanities-rooted benchmarks for cultural sensitivity, perspectival plurality, resistance to dominant attractor dynamics, and productive ambiguity preservation. Published as a standalone academic contribution with worked examples and baseline data.

WP3.2: Critical Response Guide

A companion workbook, more Gamemaster's Guide than user manual. Inspired by Liz Lerman's Critical Response Process. Rubrics and decision trees that help creators interrogate AI outputs and evaluate the quality of the co-creation dialogue. Works with any generative interpretive tool, not just ours. Comparative analysis built in.

How we measure success: We abandon output-centric metrics. No visual fidelity scores. No prompt adherence. Success is relational and performative. Does the interaction feel like a dynamic conversation? Does interpretation of inputs let the creator hold ideas in productive ambiguity? Success is when the practitioner reports that the system clarified their intent and expanded their exploratory space rather than prematurely closing it.

PROMPT 3 · THE HUMANITIES-DRIVEN TECHNICAL ADVANCE

Humanities as generative design logic

The core technical contribution is the redesign of navigation architectures in generative AI to support ambiguity, perspectival plurality, and contested representation. Rather than collapsing interpretive complexity into statistically confident single outputs.

The humanities contribution here is not post-hoc critical commentary applied after the engineering is done. It is foundational architectural constraint.

Memory studies provides the conceptual architecture. Selective distortion, the politics of forgetting, mnemonic plurality: these are operationalised as spatial properties of the latent environment and as computational design parameters. Not metaphors. Design constraints.

Narrative theory provides the compositional logic for movement through the space. Cognitive science and material engagement theory drive the reorientation from output to process, from delegation to sovereignty. The tool shapes the mind. We design for that.

PROMPT 3 · GENUINE TRANSDISCIPLINARY ENRICHMENT

Each discipline is changed

The mutual enrichment here is bidirectional and has genuine epistemic consequence.

Technical → Humanities: The implementation surfaces empirical evidence about how dominant attractors function, how communities navigate contested representational spaces, and how latent space topology encodes cultural power asymmetries. These are phenomena that are not accessible through archival or interpretive methods alone. The machine makes visible what the archive cannot.

Humanities → Technical: The requirement for computational precision and testability generates new knowledge within the humanities disciplines themselves. Concepts like selective distortion and epistemic erasure become computational design constraints rather than remaining descriptive theoretical categories. The engineering changes the theory.

This is the test: Each discipline is substantively changed by its encounter with the other. That is the condition of genuine transdisciplinary research. Not interdisciplinary collaboration in name only. Neither a humanities nor a technical AI team could achieve this independently.
